# Customer Retention Analytics

## 📊 Overview

This analysis examines subscription churn patterns to identify at-risk customer segments, quantify revenue impact, and guide retention efforts.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Churn Rate | 28.6% |
| Retention Rate | 71.4% |
| Average Tenure (Days) | 1,451 |
| ARPU | ₹18.8 |
| Total Revenue | ₹395 |
| Revenue Loss due to Churn | ₹74 |
| CLTV Lost | ₹2,047 |
| % Revenue Loss | 18% |

### Churn by Plan Type

| Plan | Churn Rate |
|---|---|
| Monthly | 55.6% |
| Annual | 8.3% |

## 🔍 Key Insights

- **Plan mix**: Most churn comes from the **Basic** subscription plan — not a major concern in terms of overall revenue impact.
- **Timing**: Churn peaked in **September 2024**.
- **Geography**: **Karnataka** is the most affected state.
- **Plan type**: Monthly plan subscribers churn at a much higher rate (55.6%) compared to annual subscribers (8.3%), suggesting commitment length is a strong retention lever.

## ✅ Action Items

- [ ] **Investigate Karnataka** — check for price increases, user complaints, or technical issues specific to the region.
- [ ] **Review Basic plan pricing** — confirm whether pricing or plan changes were made around September 2024.
- [ ] **Competitive analysis** — at least one user churned to a competitor; assess competitor offerings and pricing.
- [ ] **Prioritize high-risk customers** — segment customers by churn risk (High/Medium), rank by LTV, review complaint history, and proactively reach out via email, SMS, or calls to resolve issues.
